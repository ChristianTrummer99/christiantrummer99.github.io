the problem

according to the UN...

the average distance that women and children walk for water in rural Africa and Asia is 3.7 miles. These distances are long and arduous when transporting water by hand. Additionally, travelers are left exposed to terrors such as robbery or kidnapping. Water-focused NGOs work to bring safe drinking water to communities in developing nations by building wells and giving away water filtration devices. These have proven to be priceless additions to these water-scarce areas, but they do not address transportation issues. The WATTerWagon allows for faster transportation of much higher quantities of water. In turn, this would reduce the amount of trips to a water source and increase the speed of these trips, decreasing the risk of harm and ultimately buying time to focus on important activities such as education and income generation.

our solution

the WATTerWagon

The WATTerWagon is a solar-powered, self-charging water transportation cart that utilizes assistive motors and a responsive throttle system to quickly transport higher quantities of water. We are unique and best able to help get one step closer to ending water scarcity because of our focus on sustainability. We have a strong, evidence-based sustainable implementation model to ensure the empowerment of the communities that we will operate in, as well as able to generate favorable impact metrics for NGO stakeholders. By implementing a microbusiness model in the regions in which we operate, we will ensure that communities maintain our product without further intervention and dependency. We have also applied for a provisional patent for our unique dynamic force sensing handle, which allows for efficient battery usage by providing an assist, rather than full power that would make the operation of the device cumbersome.

our progress

since January 2019..

The WATTerWagon began as an idea in January 2019 and came to reality when it won the Quinn Prize for Best International Social Venture in the GW New Venture Competition. Since then, the WATTerWagon team has participated in a few startup accelerators and mentorship courses all while iterating on their physical designs.

More to come! Due to COVID-19, product development has been stalled, but workarounds are underway.
